2.2. The depiction sequence

Fields can be grouped into depictions, a collection of fields at a specific time. A depiction sequence is a collection of depictions that shows how the different fields change over time. A field does not have to exist in every depiction.

Figure 2.1. Depiction sequence with Normal fields

Daily and static fields are in independent time sequences since they may begin or end in the middle of a day and span over several depictions. For example, maximum temperature could be defined as the highest temperature between early morning and late evening on a particular day.

Figure 2.2. Depiction sequence with Normal, Daily and Static fields

Note that normal fields are grouped as depictions in one time sequence, but that individual daily and static fields each have separate time sequence.
